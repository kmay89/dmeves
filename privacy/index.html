Privacy, in plain language

This website is a visual companion to one teacher’s science class. Here is its entire privacy story, and the short version is: it collects nothing.

No accounts. No forms that send anywhere. No cookies. No analytics. No ads. No trackers. No server-side anything. This site is a single static page — when you visit, the page is delivered to your browser and that is the entire transaction. Nothing you do here is recorded, transmitted, or seen by anyone, including the teacher.

What stays on your own device

A few small things are saved in your browser’s local storage, on your device only, and never sent anywhere: whether you’ve already entered the class door code, whether you’ve seen the intro tour, and — if you use the teacher’s seating-chart tool — the roster and layout you typed into it. That roster exists only in the browser of the person who typed it; it is never transmitted, synced, or visible to anyone else. Anything you type into the hypothesis helper exists only while the page is open. Clearing your browser data removes all of it.

Fonts and media

The fonts are hosted on this site itself, so no font provider or CDN sees your visit. There are no embedded videos, pixels, or third-party resources of any kind.

Links to other places

Objects in the room link out to class materials — for example Google Classroom and Google Docs. Those services are provided through the school and are governed by the school’s own agreements and privacy policies, not by this site. This site neither sends information to them nor receives information from them; a link here is just a link.

Students and the law

Because this site collects, stores, and shares no personal information from anyone — student or adult — there is no student data here to protect, sell, or breach. It is designed to be safely usable by students without creating any records: nothing to be requested, subpoenaed, or leaked, because nothing exists.

Changes

If this page ever changes in a way that makes any of the above less true, the change will be visible right here, in plain language.
