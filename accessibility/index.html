Accessibility

Every student in the class should be able to use this room. Here’s what the site does toward that, and how to tell us when it falls short.

Built in

  • Keyboard everything — every object in the room, the door keypad, the legend, and all dialogs can be reached and operated with Tab, Enter, and Escape. Dialogs trap focus while open and return it when closed.
  • Screen reader labels — interactive objects are real buttons with descriptive names (“The chalkboard — syllabus and this week”); purely decorative art is hidden from assistive tech.
  • Reduced motion — if your device asks for reduced motion, the animations (flames, bubbles, orbiting planets, floating dust) hold still and dialogs appear without sliding.
  • Sound is optional — all audio is decorative, starts only after you interact, and can be switched off with the “Sound” toggle. Nothing on the site is communicated by sound alone.
  • The legend duplicates the room — everything you can click in the drawing is also a plainly labeled button beneath it, so nobody has to pixel-hunt.
  • Readable text — body text meets WCAG AA contrast on the cream background, and the layout reflows down to small phone screens.

Known limits

The room itself is one large drawing; its decorative details (the bunting, the backpack, the plant) are scenery and intentionally silent to screen readers. All actual class information is available through the labeled buttons and dialogs.

If something’s in the way

Tell the teacher — in class, or through the class’s usual channels. Barriers get fixed; that’s the whole policy.
